How It Works

The Idea

Bitcoin moves in cycles. At the top, everything looks euphoric — valuations stretched, miners printing money, sentiment greedy, funding rates sky-high. At the bottom, the opposite — holders underwater, miners capitulating, markets fearful, leverage flushed.

This score measures that shift across 23 indicators in 6 domains. Each indicator scores 0–10 based on where its current reading sits relative to its full historical range. A 0 means conditions last seen at cycle peaks. A 10 means conditions last seen at cycle lows. The weighted average gives a single composite from 0 to 100.

Historical Reference Points

Three cycle tops. Two confirmed cycle bottoms. The score landed in consistent ranges each time:

  • Cycle tops scored 5–14 — Dec 2017 (5.2), Nov 2021 (11.9), Oct 2025 (13.1). When almost nothing in the market is stressed, the score is near zero.
  • Cycle bottoms scored 70–75 — Dec 2018 (74.7), Nov 2022 (70.5). When stress is extreme across most domains, the score is high.

The current score tells you where you sit relative to those reference points.

The Data

The model pulls from 5 independent data sources — BGeometrics, Blockchain.info, Coin Metrics, Alternative.me, and Binance — updated daily via automated pipeline. Historical data spans back to 2009 for price and on-chain fundamentals, giving us over 6,000 days of raw data per core metric.

Every score shown on this page is backtested across 3,375 days (2017–present) using the exact same scoring model that runs live today. No curve-fitting to look good in hindsight — the same thresholds, the same weights, the same code.

The 6 Domains

  • On-Chain Valuation (30%) — MVRV-Z, NUPL, Realized Price, Supply in Profit, Reserve Risk, SSR, Thermocap. Is Bitcoin cheap or expensive relative to its aggregate cost basis?
  • Technical / Price (20%) — Mayer Multiple, 200-Week MA, Pi Cycle Bottom, Rainbow Chart, Drawdown. How far has price deviated from long-term norms?
  • Miner Health (15%) — Hash Ribbons, Puell Multiple. Are miners under enough pressure to capitulate?
  • Sentiment (15%) — SOPR, LTH-SOPR, Fear & Greed, Fear Duration, Funding Rates. Is the market panicking and realising losses?
  • Exchange & Liquidity (10%) — NVT Signal, Open Interest, Exchange Netflow. How is capital flowing?
  • Cycle Timing (10%) — Days since ATH, RHODL Ratio. Where are we in the typical halving cycle?

Limitations

  • Small sample. Three completed cycles. The patterns could break.
  • Correlated inputs. On-chain metrics share underlying data — this is not 23 independent signals.
  • Backward-looking. Thresholds are calibrated on past cycles. ETFs and institutional flows have changed the market.
  • Not financial advice. A research tool for cycle positioning, not a trading system.
