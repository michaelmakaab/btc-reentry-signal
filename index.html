Behind the Score — What This Model Does (and Doesn’t Do)

What This Score Actually Is

This is a bear market stress meter, not a buy signal. It measures how much simultaneous pain exists across 23 indicators spanning 6 fundamentally different measurement domains. Higher score = more stress = historically closer to a cycle bottom.

Think of it like a thermometer for market suffering. A high reading tells you the market is very sick. It does not tell you the fever has peaked.

What It’s Good At (Backed by Data)

  • Cycle top avoidance — 3 for 3. At every major peak (Dec 2017 at $19K, Nov 2021 at $67K, Oct 2025 at $125K), the score was deep in No Entry (4–8 out of 100). It has never signalled stress during a top. If the score is low, it is not time to buy the dip.
  • Strong Buy bottom timing — nailed it (n=1). The only time the score crossed 75 was Dec 2018, within 8% of the absolute cycle bottom at $3,128. Returns: +120% at 6 months, +271% peak within one year. However, this has only happened once in the entire dataset.
  • Higher score = lower price within bear markets. During 2022, days with score 50–55 averaged $21.8K while days at score 65–70 averaged $18.7K. The score does rank-order relative value within a bear market, even if it can’t pinpoint the exact bottom.

What It’s NOT Good At (Also Backed by Data)

  • The “High Stress” zone (50–75) is not a buy signal. In Jun 2022, the score crossed 50 at $26,593. The actual bottom came 5 months later at $15,742 — another 41% down. A simple rule of “buy at -75% from ATH” would have entered at ~$16K and outperformed. The score read ~67 at both $20K in June and $16K at the actual bottom — it couldn’t tell the difference.
  • It doesn’t consistently beat random bear market buying. We compared the Jun 2022 High Stress entry ($26.6K) against the average price on random days during that bear market ($21.1K). Random buying was 26% cheaper. The model detected the bear market regime correctly, but didn’t improve on simply buying randomly during it.
  • The score can’t distinguish intermediate crashes from real bottoms. The composite gave essentially the same reading (~67) during the Jun 2022 crash and the Nov 2022 actual bottom. Multiple indicators “look bottomy” at any deep drawdown, not just the final one.

How to Read the Zones

  • 0–25 No Entry — Market is not under stress. Not a bottoming environment. This is where the model has its strongest track record — every cycle top registered here.
  • 25–50 Watching — Some stress building. Prepare capital and monitor, but no rush.
  • 50–75 High Stress — Bear market pain is widespread. You are likely in a bear market. But this does NOT mean the bottom is in. The Jun 2022 signal saw -36% more downside over 6 months. Treat this as a regime indicator, not a timing signal. If you act here, use small positions with DCA.
  • 75–100 Strong Buy — Extreme cross-domain confluence. Fired once (Dec 2018), within 8% of the bottom. If it fires again, it warrants attention — but remember n=1.

Benchmark: Score vs Simple Drawdown Rules

For honesty, here’s how the model’s High Stress (50+) signals compare against buying at a fixed percentage below the all-time high:

  • 2018 cycle: High Stress first triggered at $5,757 (-71% from ATH). A -75% drawdown rule would have entered at ~$4,500. Bottom was $3,128.
  • 2022 cycle: High Stress first triggered at $26,593 (-61% from ATH). A -75% drawdown rule would have entered at ~$16K. Bottom was $15,742. The simple rule won by a wide margin: 1-year return of +131% vs -3% for the model’s signal.

The model adds genuine value at the Strong Buy level (75+) and for cycle top avoidance. The High Stress zone tells you the market is in pain, but a simple -75% drawdown rule has historically been a better entry timer.

The Model: 6 Domains, 23 Indicators

  • On-Chain Valuation (30%) — MVRV-Z, NUPL, Realized Price, Supply in Profit, Reserve Risk, Stablecoin Supply Ratio, Thermocap Multiple. Compares price to aggregate cost basis. Note: these are partially correlated (all derive from the realized cap) and are grouped as one domain, not treated as independent.
  • Technical / Price (20%) — Mayer Multiple, 200-Week MA, Pi Cycle Bottom, Rainbow Chart, drawdown depth. Mean-reversion measures of how far price has deviated from long-term norms.
  • Miner Health (15%) — Hash Ribbons (30d vs 60d hash rate cross) and Puell Multiple (daily revenue vs 365d avg). Miner capitulation removes a persistent sell pressure source.
  • Sentiment (15%) — SOPR, LTH-SOPR, Fear & Greed, Fear Duration, Funding Rates. Measures whether the market is in panic and sellers are realizing losses.
  • Exchange & Liquidity (10%) — NVT Signal, Open Interest, Exchange Netflow. Lower weight because exchange data is less reliable in the ETF era.
  • Cycle Timing (10%) — Days since ATH relative to historical bottoming windows, RHODL Ratio. Lowest weight — no fundamental reason future cycles must follow the same timing.

Each indicator is scored 0–10 (10 = historically confirmed bottom levels). Domain averages are weighted and summed to a 0–100 composite. If a domain has no data, its weight is redistributed to active domains.

Honest Limitations

  • Tiny sample size (n=3). Three complete cycles is not enough to validate any model statistically. This could easily be overfit.
  • Retroactive backtest. Thresholds and weights were tuned on historical data. This is not out-of-sample.
  • Correlated indicators. On-chain metrics share underlying data. It’s not 23 independent signals.
  • Regime change. ETFs, institutional flows, and regulatory changes mean past cycle dynamics may not repeat.
  • High Stress ≠ bottom. The Jun 2022 and Feb 2026 signals both demonstrate that elevated stress can precede months of further decline.
  • Not financial advice. This is a research framework, not a trading system.
